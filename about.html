About MetaStrip

Our Mission

MetaStrip was created with one clear goal: to help people protect their privacy when sharing photos online. In a world where digital privacy is increasingly important, we wanted to provide a simple, free tool that removes hidden metadata from images without requiring technical knowledge.

Privacy-First Philosophy

We believe your data belongs to you. That's why MetaStrip processes everything locally in your browser. Your photos never leave your device, and we don't collect any personal information from you or your images.

The Problem We're Solving

Every time you take a photo with your smartphone or digital camera, it automatically embeds information like:

  • The exact GPS coordinates where the photo was taken
  • The date and time the photo was taken
  • Details about your camera or smartphone model
  • Technical settings used to take the photo
  • Sometimes even your name or the serial number of your device

When you share these photos online, all of this information goes with them, potentially exposing personal details about your life, habits, and location to anyone who knows how to extract this data.

Key Features

100% Private Processing

All processing happens in your browser. Your photos never leave your device, ensuring complete privacy.

User-Friendly Interface

Simple drag-and-drop functionality makes it easy for anyone to use, regardless of technical expertise.

Detailed Metadata Viewing

See exactly what information is embedded in your photos before deciding to remove it.

Complete Metadata Removal

Strip all EXIF metadata with one click, giving you a clean image safe for sharing.

Free to Use

MetaStrip is and always will be completely free to use. No registration required, no hidden fees.

No Server Dependencies

The tool works entirely client-side, so you can even save it and use it offline if needed.

Future Plans

We're constantly working to improve MetaStrip. Some features we're planning to add include:

  • Support for additional image formats beyond JPEG
  • Batch processing for multiple images
  • Selective metadata removal (keeping copyright info while removing location data)
  • Additional visualization options for location data
  • Educational resources about metadata and digital privacy

If you have suggestions for features you'd like to see, please contact us!
